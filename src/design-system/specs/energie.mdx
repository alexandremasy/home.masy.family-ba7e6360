# Énergie — spec

Monthly meter readings (eau, électricité jour/nuit, citerne mazout), their history, and a
contextual "à saisir" prompt.

## Purpose

Track household consumption from manually-entered monthly readings, surface the trend, and
nudge when the current month's reading is missing.

## Behaviour

- The domain is **contextual**: a warm "Relevé mensuel à saisir" banner shows only when no
  reading exists for the current month (`isMonthlyDue`). Otherwise a quiet info bar shows the
  last reading + a "Nouveau relevé" affordance.
- Entering a reading (`/energie/saisie`) upserts the four meters for a date, then **must
  invalidate the energy queries** so the banner clears immediately (queries have a 1h
  staleTime — skipping invalidation is why the banner used to persist after entry).
- Oil is a level/tank meter (autonomy in days); the others are cumulative counters (delta =
  consumption between readings).

## States

- **Due** — no current-month reading → warm banner.
- **Done** — reading present → info bar with last date + covered month.
- **History** — per-month charts (elec kWh, water m³, oil liters) + an editable relevés table.

## Data contract

- Source of truth is the **api** (`/v1/energy`), never the mockup. Reads: `/summary`,
  `/meters`, `/readings`, per-meter `consumption`. Writes: `POST /readings` (idempotent upsert
  on meter+date).
- `isMonthlyDue(rows)` = latest reading predates the current month.
- Presentation (formatting, alert thresholds like oil < 25%) lives in the consumer, not the api.
