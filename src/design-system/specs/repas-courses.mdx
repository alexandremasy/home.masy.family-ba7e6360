# Repas · Courses — spec

The shopping list is **opt-in per meal**, not auto-derived. This is the decision that kept
getting re-litigated — it lives here now.

## Purpose

Turn the meal plan into a shopping list the household actually builds, and mirror it to
Google Tasks so it's usable at the store.

## Behaviour

- Adding a meal to the plan adds **nothing** to the list.
- Clicking a component pill on a meal opts **that meal's** contribution in. Quantity is the
  **sum** of the included meals, so the same ingredient (poulet in the wrap _and_ in the
  salad) is independent and accumulates.
- Removing a meal's pill deducts only its share; the others remain.
- Editing a quantity directly in the list is a manual override. Setting a line to **0**
  clears that ingredient's inclusions across every meal.
- Staples are always on the list; manual lines can be added by hand.

## States

- **Empty** — a planned week with nothing opted in yet (an empty card invites adding, not a
  filter button).
- **Built** — lines grouped by aisle, quantities summed from included meals + staples + manual.
- **Acquired** — checking a line off (or completing its Google task) removes it from the list.

## Data contract

- Source of truth is the **api** (`/v1/repas`), never the mockup. The list is **server-derived**:
  `loadCourseList(from, to)` = plan + dishes + staples merged with `repas_course_line_state`,
  filtered by per-meal `repas_course_inclusions` (`plan_entry_id` + `name|unit`).
- Natural id of a line: `` `${name}|${unit}` ``.
- Google Tasks sync is **bidirectional** (title = `"Nom · qty unit"`, no technical note);
  completing a task there clears the line here.

## Open questions

- Should staples be per-household-configurable, or stay a fixed list?
- Multi-store split (e.g. pharmacy vs grocery) — out of scope for now.
